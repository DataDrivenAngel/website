The verdict NO TORQUE

Recoil is felt at the breech, not the barrel. The breech crossbar carries current antiparallel to the armature, so the two repel: the armature is shoved toward the muzzle and the breech is shoved backward by an equal force. The rails themselves only feel sideways "blowout" — they contribute essentially nothing to the axial recoil.

With the breech on the bore axis, the launch force and the recoil force are collinear → they cancel as a straight-back kick with no net couple. The gun slides back without yawing.

Honest fine print for the pedants: the EM forces are internal, so the closed circuit can't spin itself up — net force and net torque over the entire loop are ≈0 (see "Net force" above). The twist appears the instant the projectile separates: the structure is left with a backward push applied at the offset breech, off the bore line → a couple τ = F·d. How fast it actually rotates depends on the gun's moment of inertia and where it's mounted; the turning moment itself is unambiguous. Same effect as a cannon whose recoil rod sits off the bore axis → muzzle climb.

Method: each conductor discretised into filament elements; B from every other element via Biot–Savart (μ₀/4π=1e-7), F = I·dL×B. Forces scale as I². Idealised thin filaments — directions & ratios are exact, absolute magnitudes are order-of-magnitude.
