The verdict NO TORQUE

Recoil is felt at the breech / junction, not the muzzle. Whatever crossbar closes the circuit carries current antiparallel to the armature, so the two repel: armature shoved toward the muzzle, the closure shoved backward by an equal force. The rails only feel sideways "blowout."

With the junction on the bore axis, launch and recoil are collinear → straight-back kick, no couple.

Flexible-cable note: routing the return out to an offset junction does not change the launch force (it's set by the rails at the armature) and the system still recoils with equal-and-opposite momentum. What changes is where the reaction lands and how it reaches the barrel. The big backward reaction now sits on the junction and the cables; because the cables are flexible they transmit only tension along their length (and no bending moment), so the barrel feels recoil as a rearward tug at the cable lugs. Vertical offset makes the two cable tensions asymmetric → a genuine twist on the barrel. Pure setback (no offset) just enlarges the loop and dumps enormous Lorentz load into the cables — opposite-current cables repel, so long ones whip and try to fly apart.

Method: each conductor discretised into filament elements; B from every other element via Biot–Savart (μ₀/4π=1e-7) with finite-radius softening, F = I·dL×B. Forces scale as I². Directions & ratios are robust; absolute magnitudes are order-of-magnitude. Cables drawn dashed = flexible.
